One of the best things you could do to increase your productivity along with improving the quality of your code is to create your own **styleguide**. It could be personal or a team's styleguide.

Either way, it will help you to enforce the *uniformity of your codebase* and to spend less time thinking about the way you will write the next line of code. Thus allowing you to spend more time on more important things like architecture, naming etc. It will definitely help with *code reviews* as well by having a single source of authority.

Since styleguide is a broad term, it could pertain not only to the style of the code itself but to the whole process of development starting from gathering input all the way to deployment and managing servers.

Maybe you can even call it a Code of Conduct and force team's newcomers to take an oath on it :)

A good idea is to use other styleguides as a reference and only choose parts that you like mixing it with your own preferences.

For Ruby projects, you can easily put some of the rules you created into a static code analyzer like *rubocop* or *reek*.

Some of the guides to draw inspiration from:

- <http://shopify.github.io/ruby-style-guide>
- <https://github.com/infinum/rails-handbook>
- <https://github.com/thoughtbot/guides>
- <https://github.com/bbatsov/ruby-style-guide>
- <https://github.com/airbnb/ruby>
